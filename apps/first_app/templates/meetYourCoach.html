Get To Know Your Coach

  • 1974-1977

    Organized Running Begins

    I ran track in high school, where I established my best times of 4:32 and 51.3 in the mile and quarter mile, respectively.

  • 1980-1986

    Caught the Marathon Bug

    In 1980 I made my marathon debut at the Marine Corp Marathon with a time of 3:23. From 1983 to 1986 I competed in road races while in the Army and stationed in Hawaii with personal records of 16:22 for the 5k, 35:00 for the 10k, and 1:19 for the half marathon.

  • 1987-1990

    Coached At the High School Level

    I coached track and field at the high school level in Burlington, Massachusetts at Burlington High School. In 1988 I snuck in a marathon personal record at the Boston Peace Marathon, with a time of 3:03.

  • 1993-2010

    Michigan Living, Susan G. Komen Race for the Cure

    While living in Battle Creek, Michigan (Cereal City, USA!) I raised my three daughters and continued to run. I made a personal record at a 10-mile race with a time of 1:05. I started a Susan G. Komen Race for the Cure event in Battle Creek in 1996, and served as Race Chair and Director for 5 years. I was the first male Race Chairman in the country. I served on the local Komen Board of Directors from 1998 to 2004, and I represented all Komen Affiliates on the National Board of Directors in 1999. In my last year in Battle Creek, I worked as the director of the Cancer Care Center at Battle Creek Health Systems and was appointed a board member of the Michigan Cancer Consortium.

  • 2010-Present

    Abu Dhabi Calling

    In 2010 I moved to Abu Dhabi in the United Arab Emirates to work at New York University Abu Dhabi in the Department of Athletics. In 2012 I became a certified coach with USA Track and Field, Road Runners Clubs of America, and USA Triathlon. In 2016 I had a total knee replacement, but I continue to run with a recent time of 22:58 in the 5k at 58 years old. Overall I've completed 11 marathons over the years, qualifying for and running Boston twice at 40 and 50 years old.
