"I was an undergraduate student at NYUAD from 2012-2016. I had run cross-country in high school, so I was not a beginner, but Coach Wayne was very welcoming to everyone, and highly encouraged everyone to run; he always said, "You don't have to be a runner to run. Everyone can run-- it's just practice that makes you better." There are many running opportunities at NYUAD that Coach Wayne advertised, and I was eager to jump on board many of them (ie. Wadih Bih, monthly road races, indoor triathlons, a half-marathon in Cyprus, and more). I, personally, really benefited from his weekly Sunday Track Workouts. It can be really difficult to motivate yourself to design and execute tough workouts by yourself, so I really looked forward to this intense weekly session that he organized. I liked that Coach Wayne was there to explain the workout, keep track of everything (splits, rest time, intervals, etc), yell at us to pick up the pace or to shout encouraging thoughts from across the track, and share with us countless running tips as we gasped for air in between sets. I enjoyed running hard with a group under his watch, and I could see myself improving (getting stronger, faster, and/or better technique) week by week thanks to his workouts. As much as I enjoyed them, what really stuck with me is Coach Wayne's commitment to these weekly track workouts. Regardless of how many people would show up, Coach Wayne would be out on the track every Sunday at the given hour. During exam time or particularly busy times during the school year sometimes there would only be one or two people who would come to his sessions. One time, I was running late and was surprised to see no one on the track-- no one except Coach Wayne that is. Even though I was the only person, he gave me a workout to do and stayed with me to watch my form and instruct my pace and sets. He was always very supportive of all his runners and had a good eye for observing running form. He would sometimes even videotape us and play the recording back in slow motion to show us exactly what we could fix. Another one of the things I will never forget about Coach Wayne is his love of the megaphone. Coach Wayne was a volunteer for just about every road race I raced in in Abu Dhabi, and he always had the "announcer" role, so I got to hear his encouraging voice narrate my run as I passed by, along with some additional words of motivation. To me, his megaphone volunteering at early morning races exemplified both his dedication to the sport of running as well as his commitment to building (being a big contributor) to his community, and it showcased his cheery personality. Besides being a great running coach from a technical standpoint, what makes Coach Wayne a truly beloved coach is the relationships he fosters with his runners. I always felt at ease when he was around, that he was supportive and welcoming, and that he had an open door policy such that I could just walk into his office and talk about anything with him. I would also like to add that I think Coach Wayne has a great sense of humor."

Tamara Sanchez Ortiz
